The Vocative Case

The vocative is the case by which one addresses or calls out to someone. It is generally very similar in form to the nominative. Notice there is no definite article for it in the table. That is because there is no definite article for the vocative, though often it is preceeded by the interjection ὦ.

ὦ Ζεῦ, ὁ βίος ἐστὶ χαλεπὸς.
O Zeus, life is difficult.
