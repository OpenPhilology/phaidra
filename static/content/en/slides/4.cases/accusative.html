The Accusative Case

Accusative for Direct Objects

The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. A direct object is the thing being acted upon by the subject.

ὁ Θουκυδίδης ἐξηγέται τόν πόλεμον.
Thucydides describes the war.
(Subject) (Verb) (Direct Object)
ἡ πόλις ἔχει στρατίαν.
The city has an army.
(Subject) (Verb) (Direct Object)
Don't forget!
While there is a Greek transaltion for the definite article "the", there is no translation for the indefinite article "a" or "an". In this case, you can just skip the Greek article and translate the noun directly.
