Adverbs

Most adjectives in Ancient Greek are formed by adding -ως to the stem. Remember the stem is found by dropping the ending of the masculine genitive singular! The following are a few examples of the process of deriving an adverb from an adjective.

Nominative Masculine Singular Genitive Masculine Singular Adverb
δίκαιος (just) δικαίου δικαίως (justly)
ἄδικος (unjust) ἀδίκου ἀδίκως (unjustly)
πᾶς (whole) παντός πάντως (wholly)
ἡδύς (sweet) ἡδέος ἡδέως (sweetly)
