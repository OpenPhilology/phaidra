Digraphs in Greek

These so-called "γ nasals" are example of digraphs in Ancient Greek. Di, meaning 'two', and graph meaning 'something written'.

Letter combination Pronunciation Example Pronunciation
γγ ng ἄγγελος angelos
γκ nk ἄγκυρα anküra
γχ nkh λόγχη lonkhe
γξ nx σάλπιγξ salpinx
You don't need to know what they're called, just remember that if a γ is followed by another γ, or a κ, χ, ξ, you should pronounce the first γ as though it were actually a ν.
