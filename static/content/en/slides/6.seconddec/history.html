The Athenian wall

The wall of Athens was built in 479 BC with the help of all its citizens, including women and children.

...previously in the Pentecontaetia

The Greeks returned home after the war and the Athenians rebuilt their city wall. Themistocles went to Sparta to cover up the truth about it.

Now in the Pentecontaetia...

Themistocles lies to οἱ Λακεδαιμόνιοι, the Spartans, about the rebuilding of the wall, and though wanting to believe him for the sake of friendship, the Spartans are suspicious enough to send their men to Athens to check. Meanwhile, ὁ Θεμιστοκλῆς, Themistocles is joined by two colleagues bringing the news that the wall of Athens has been rebuilt. Themistocles decides to break the news to the Spartans and tells them that the wall is only one of the measures that Athens is taking to protects its own interests. The Spartans listen and accept Themistocles' words with outward grace, but secret annoyance...

(Summary of Thuc. 1.91-92)
