Second declension continued...

And now here is an example of a neuter second declension noun: τό ἔργον meaning the work.

Neuter Nouns are a Special Case

In all neuter nouns, the accusative and the vocative are the same as the nominative. This works for both singular and plural.

Examples

Notice how τὸ χωρίον remains in the same form in these two sentences, even though it is the object of the first sentence and the subject in the second.

Neuter noun in the Accusative

βίᾳ γὰρ ἂν εἷλον τὸ χωρίον.

For otherwise they would have taken the place by force. (Thuc 1.102.4)

Neuter noun in the Nominative

...ἐπειδὴ τὸ χωρίον βίᾳ οὐχ ἡλίσκετο...

...but since the place was not conquered by force... (Thuc 1.102.6)
