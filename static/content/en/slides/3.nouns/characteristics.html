Identifying the 3 characteristics of nouns

  1. To identify the gender of a noun, look at its definite article.
  2. To identify the number of a noun, look at its definite article and its ending.
  3. To identify the case of a noun, look at its definite article, stem and ending.

Let's take the Greek word for "day" again:

ἡ (the) ἡμέρα (Day)
ἡ
ἡμέρ
α
Definite Article
The definite article, ἡ ("the"), tells us ἡμέρα is a feminine (gender) noun. 		Stem
The stem, ἡμέρ-, stays constant as we change the noun's number and case. 		Ending
The ending, -α, indicates this is a singular (number) nominative (case) noun.
This means that ἡ ἡμέρα is a Feminine, Singular, Nominative noun.
