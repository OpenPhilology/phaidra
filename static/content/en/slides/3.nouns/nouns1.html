Characteristics of Greek Nouns

Every Greek noun will have these 3 important characteristics:

  1. Gender: Masculine, Feminine, or Neuter
  2. Number: Singular or Plural
  3. Case: Nominative, Genitive, Dative, Accusative or Vocative

Cases in English

While Gender and Number may be easy concepts to grasp, Case can be tricky for uninflected-language speakers. Case indicates the change in the ending of a word according to its function in the sentence. Though English is largely uninflected, some words do change according to their function.

Example

Who am I?

"Who" is in the nominative case, because it is the subject.

I have seen the man whom I admire.

"Whom" is in accusative case, because it is the direct object.

Here, English adds an "m" to indicate how "who" functions in the sentence. With an "m", it's the direct object, without, it's the subject.

English also does this with personal pronouns: She walks the dog (subject), or does the dog walk her? (direct object)
