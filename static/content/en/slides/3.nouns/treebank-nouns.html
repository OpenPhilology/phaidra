Building Parse Trees for Nouns

What's a parse tree, you ask?

A parse tree is a way to represent that you understand the grammar of a greek sentence. If one word depends on another, it goes below it on the tree. Simple as pie. Read on to learn your first rule of dependency!

[ { "id": 1, "head": 2, "relation": "ATR", "value": "οἱ", "pos": "article" }, { "id": 2, "head": 0, "relation": "SBJ", "value": "Ἀθηναῖοι", "pos": "noun" } ]

Try it yourself!

  1. Click on οἱ ("the"), the dependent.
  2. Click on Ἀθηναῖοι ("Athenians"), the parent.
  3. Profit.

Wait, what did I just do?

You just demonstrated you understand that in this fragment, οἱ Ἀθηναῖοι, the Athenians, belong together because:

  • οἱ depends upon Ἀθηναῖοι, because they agree in Case, Number, and Gender (both are Masculine, Plural, Nominative).

That's it! Here's your first rule to learn on your quest for parse tree proficiency:

1. Whenever you see a definite article and a noun that it agrees with, that definite article should be directly below it on a parse tree.

Keep that in mind as you try the next couple exercises.
