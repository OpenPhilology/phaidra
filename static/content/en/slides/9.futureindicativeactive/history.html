Thucydides

I am the only historian who has described the growth of Athenian power accurately!

...previously in the Pentecontaetia

The Athenians formed an anti-Persian alliance.

Now in the Pentecontaetia...

The power of the Athenians grows exponentially through their military and political actions against the barbarians, against their own allies in revolt and against the Peloponnesians. Athens' new alliance attacks locations, such as Eion, Scyros, Carystus and Naxos, whose citizens initiate a revolt against the confederacy.

οἱ Ἀθηναῖοι, the Athenians, grow more and more unpopular as they demand monetary and military contributions from the allies. For this, οἱ ξύμμαχοι, the allies, have themselves to blame; wishing to get off service, they had decided to pay their share in money instead of in ships. Thus the Athenian navy is growing richer than ever before.

(Summary of Thuc. 1.97-98-99)
