Second Accent Rule: Acute Accent

  1. The acute accent, like other accents, can never must be placed one one of the last three syllables of a word.
  2. It can be found over the antepenult only if the last syllable is short.

This example shows how the acute accent of this word is placed on the antepenult (ἄν) because the ultima (πος) is short.

For the Ultima to be long, it would need to contain a long vowel, such as ω.
