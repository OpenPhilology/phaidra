Second Accent Rule: Acute Accent

The acute accent can never be placed on any syllable before the antepenult, but only on the antepenult, penult or ultima. It can be found over the antepenult only if the last syllable is short.

For example:
ἄν
θρω
πος
ANTEPENULT
 PENULT
 ULTIMA

This example shows how the acute accent of this word is placed on the antepenult because the ultima is short.
