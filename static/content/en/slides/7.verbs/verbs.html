Conjugating verbs in English is relatively simple. In Greek, the verbs themselves, instead of a subject like "I" or "You", commonly indicates who is performing the action of the verb.

How Greek Verbs work

Just like nouns, Greek verbs are inflected and just like nouns they add an ending to their stem to produce their various forms. While the patterns for nouns are called declensions (and the noun is declined), the verb forms fall into conjugations (so the verb is conjugated).

Most Greek verbs will have these 5 important characteristics:

  1. Person
  2. Number
  3. Tense
  4. Mood
  5. Voice
How does Greek differ from English?

Unlike English verbs, the subject of the Greek verb is implied by the verb's ending. Therefore, it is not necessary for it to be accompanied by the personal pronouns I, You, He/She/It, We, You, They.

For example: The English I have can be translated either ἐγώ ἔχω or, more commonly, simply ἔχω.
