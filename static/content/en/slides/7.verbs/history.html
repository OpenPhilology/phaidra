Pausanias, the military leader of Sparta


I am most famous for leading the Hellenes during the decisive battle of Plataea against the Persians.

...previously in the Pentecontaetia

Themistocles revealed to the Spartans the truth about the wall.

Now in the Pentecontaetia...

The wall is completed in a very short period of time, for the foundations are laid with stones of all kinds, pieces of columns, tombs, and sculptures. ὁ Θεμιστοκλῆς, Themistocles, insists that the Piraeus, the port of Athens, is also fortified. He, in fact, can foresee the great power that Athens will gain by being a city of naval people.

Meanwhile, while the Spartans are still supreme among οἱ Ἕλληνες, the Hellenes, the Spartan military leader Pausanias leads τό ναυτικόν, a fleet of fifty Greek ships and conquers Cyprus and Byzantium.

(Summary of Thuc. 1.93-94)
