Imperfect Indicative Active

To form the Imperfect Indicative Active:

  1. Take the present tense form, also known as the first "principle part."
    e.g. λύω
  2. Remove the ending (-ω) to get the the present stem.
    e.g. λύ-
  3. Add a ἐ- in front. This prefix is called the "Past Indicative Augment".
    e.g. ἔλυ-
  4. Add the endings from the table to indicate person and number. Notice that these are different than the ending you use for the present and future tenses!
    e.g. ἔλυον, I was untying — or — ἔλυομεν, We were untying.

Here's a Clue!

Be on the look out for verbs that begin with an ἐ. When a verb gets an ἐ as a prefix, this ἐ is called the past indicative augment because it only occurs when: 1 The verb indicates a past action 2 The verb is in the indicative mood.

If you see a verb without the Past Indicative Augument, this is a good sign that it's in another tense and voice.
