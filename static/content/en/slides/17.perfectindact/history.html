...previously in the Pentecontaetia

The Athenians failed to conquer Thessaly and Acarnia, but were successful in Sicyon.

Now in the Pentecontaetia...

After three years of battling, the Athenians and the Peloponnesians make a truce. The Athenians, now free from the Hellenic war, decide to send 200 vessels towars Cyprus, 60 of which headed to Egypt. The ships are lead by Κίμων, Cimon, who lays siege at Citium. Cimon dies shortly after, due to lack of provisions and the whole army leaves Citium and reunites with the vessels who had gone to Egypt. During their return they are attacked by Phoenicians, Cyprians and Cilicians, but they manage to defeat them!

In the meantime, the Spartans become masters of Delphi, but are soon sent away by the Athenians!

(Summary of Thuc. 1.112)
