Perfect Indicative Active

To form the perfect indicative active:

  1. Take the present tense form, also known as the first "principle part".
    e.g. λύω
  2. Remove the ending (-ω) to get the the present stem.
    e.g. λύ-
    3. Add the augment ε (just like you did for the imperfect and aorist).
  3. Add the first letter of the stem in front of the augment.
    e.g. λέλυ-
  4. Add the suffix -κ.
    e.g. λέλυκ-.
  5. Add the the endings of the present indicative active, except for the third person singular, where the ending can be either -ε or -ο, depending on the verb.
    e.g. λέλυκα, meaning I have untied.

The perfect then becomes the fourth principle part of a verb.
