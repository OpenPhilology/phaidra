...previously in the Pentecontaetia

The Athenians established themselves as masters of the river Nile.

Now in the Pentecontaetia...

War breaks out between Athens and the island opposite, Aegina. The Athenians win a great sea battle against the island and take seventy of Aegina's ships. Desirous to help the Aeginetans, the Peloponnesians send 300 hoplites to their aid.

Meanwhile, Κίμωνοἱ Κορίνθιοι, the Corithians march down to attack Athens, believing most of their army employed in Aegina. However, after a drawn battle, the Athenians defeat the Corinthians, who retreat home.

(Summary of Thuc. 1.105-106)
