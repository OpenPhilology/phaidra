Aorist Indicative Active: Second Aorist

In the third principle part of the verbs you have seen so far, the ending for the first person singular has always been -α. This is becasue the verbs you have seen so far have first aorists. There are, however, some verbs with second aorists. For these the endings change, despite the meaning remaining the same as first aorists.

For example, in the case of λείπω, to leave, the aorist indicative active is ἔλιπον. The endings of the second aorist active resemble those of the imperfect.
