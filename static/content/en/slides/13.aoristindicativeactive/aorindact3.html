Aorist Indicative Active

The aorist can have many different endings. The general rule, however, is that the aorist can be either sigmatic (meaning with an added σ) or asigmatic (without σ).

To form the sigmatic aorist indicative active:

  1. Take the present tense form, also known as the first "principle part".
    e.g. λύω
  2. Remove the ending (-ω) to get the the present stem.
    e.g. λύ-
    3. Add the augment ἐ (just like you did for the imperfect).
  3. Add a σ to get the sigmatic aorist stem.
    e.g. ἔλυσ-
  4. Add the endings from the table to indicate the person and number.
    e.g. ἐλύσαμεν, We untied

The aorist then becomes the third principle part of a verb.
