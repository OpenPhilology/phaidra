Aorist Indicative Active for Stop Verbs

The aorist stem of many verbs simply ends in σ, as you just learned.

But sometimes, when adding the σ to the end, it combines with the final letter of the present stem to form another letter. Take a look at the three categories below:



κ or γ or χ + σ ξ

For example:

λέγω, I say

ἔλεξα, I said

π or β or φ + σ ψ

For example:

πέμπω, I send

ἔπεμψα, I sent

τ or δ or θ + σ σ

For example:

πείθω, I persuade

ἔπεισα, I persuaded
