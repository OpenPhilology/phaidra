How First and Second Declension Adjectives work

Just like articles and nouns have gender, number and case, so do all the adjectives. Like the article, an adjective must agree in gender, number and case with the noun it describes.

However, this does not mean that the ending of the article and that of the noun are always identical; there are exceptions where, though the adjective and noun agree, the endings might look different.

Adjectives agreeing with third declension nouns fall into two groups:

  • Three-ending Adjectives
    These can have three types of endings:
    • -ας -ασα
      • -αν
    • -ας -αινα
      • -αν
    • -ύς -εῖα
      • -ύ



  • Two-ending Adjectives
    These can have two types of endings:
    • -ων for masculine and feminine; -ον
      • for neuter
    • -ης for masculine and feminine; -ες
      • for neuter
