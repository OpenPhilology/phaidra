Building Parse Trees for Nouns

What's the final step?

In order to build complete basic trees, the last thing you should learn about it are adverbials (ADV). ADVs specify the circumstances under which an event expressed by a verb takes place. An ADV can be an adverb, a prepositional phrase, participle or subordinate clause. In a tree the ADV will depend on the word to which it is referred. Remember, ADV are always optional constituents, in the sense that a sentence can have some meaning even without them (which does not mean that they are not important, but that you can conceive of a context where they might be left out).
