Vocabulary

Σικελία, ᾶς, ἡ Sicily.
ἡμέρᾱ, ᾱς, ἡ day.
συμμαχία, ᾱς, ἡ alliance offensive and defensive.
ναυμαχία, ᾱς, ἡ sea-fight.
στρατιά, ᾶς, ἡ army.
χώρᾱ, ᾱς, ἡ place, land, country.
συμφορά, ᾱς, ἡ literally, this simply describes an event, something that happens , but it often refers to a bad event.
αἰτία, ᾱς, ἡ charge, accusation.
ἡσυχία, ᾱς, ἡ stillness, rest, quiet.
ὠφελία T, ᾱς, ἡ help, aid.
σωτηρία, ᾱς, ἡ saving, deliverance, preservation, safety.
βία, ᾱς, ἡ bodily strength, force, power, might.
ἐκκλησία, ᾱς, ἡ assembly of citizens.
οἰκία, ᾱς, ἡ building, house, dwelling.
ὀλιγαρχία, ᾱς, ἡ oligarchy, government by a few families or persons.
μακρά adj., long.
μῑκρά adj., small, little.
ἐν prep. with dat., in (a proclitic).
ἦν he (she, it) was;
ἦσαν they were.
ἔχει he (she, it) has;
ἔχουσι they have.

The gender of a noun is indicated by placing after it the corresponding form of the definite article, as ἀγορά, ᾶς, ἡ, where ἡ singnifies that ἀγορά is feminine.
