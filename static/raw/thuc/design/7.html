Nominatives in Thucydides

The predicate adjective of a sentence belongs in the nominative case. Remember, a predicate adjective is an adjective that describes a noun, linked to it with a helping verb such as 'to be' or 'become', which is also in the nominative case.

ἦν τε ἡ ναυμαχία1 καρτερά2, τῇ μὲν τέχνῃ οὐχ ὁμοίως, πεζομαχίᾳ δὲ τὸ πλέον προσφερὴς οὖσα.

Thucydides' History of the Peloponnesian War, 1.049.2
The sea-fight1 was obstinate2, though not remarkable for its science; indeed it was more like a battle by land.

Important things to notice!
  1. Both ναυμαχία and καρτερά are Nominative, Singular, Feminine in their characteristics, even though they are a noun and an adjective, respectively.
  2. This sentence links the subject and adjective with "was", which explains why they are both in the nominative case.
