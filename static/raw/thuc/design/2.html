More on Number

Number operates in Greek similarly to the way it works in English. Usually, English words gain an -s to pluralize a word. Greek, however, creates plurals based on the declension of the noun.

Declension Singular Plural
2nd Declension
Masculine 		ὁ λόγος
the word 		οἱ λόγοι
the words
1st Declension
Feminine 		ἡ ἡμέρα
the day 		αἱ ἡμέραι
the days
2nd Declension
Neuter 		τό ἔργον
the work 		τὰ ἔργᾰ
the works

In order to properly change the number of a word, you must know both the declension and the gender of the noun. Whenever you learn a noun, you must also learn the declension and gender. Most of the time it will be obvious as long as you memorize the dictionary entry for the word.
