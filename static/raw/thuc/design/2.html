More on Number

Number operates in Greek similarly to the way it works in English. Usually, English words gain an -s to pluralize a word. Greek, however, creates plurals based on the declension of the noun.

Singular Plural
ἡ ἡμέρα
the day 		αἱ ἡμέραι
the days

For nouns like ἡ ἡμέρα, the definite article ἡ.

When a noun changes its number, from singular to plural or vice verse, both the definite article and the ending change.
