How did we know?

When you see a noun, you can look at its three main parts to find out its gender, number, and case. For example:

ἡ (the) ἡμέρα (Day)
ἡ
ἡμέρ
α
Definite Article
The definite article, ἡ ("the"), tells us ἡμέρα is a feminine (gender) noun. 		Stem
The stem, ἡμέρ-, stays constant as we change the noun's number and case. 		Ending
The ending, -α, indicates this is a singular (number) nominative (case) noun.
This means, ἡ ἡμέρα is a Nominative, Singular, Feminine noun.

Whenever you see a noun, you should be able to deduce these three characteristics.
