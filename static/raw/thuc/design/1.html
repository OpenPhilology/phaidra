More on Gender

Every noun in Greek is considered to be of feminine, masculine, or neuter gender. The gender of a noun is an aspect of the word's grammar, and is not the same as biological sex.

You can identify the gender of a noun in two main ways:

  1. The definite article ("the") which preceeds the word.
  2. The ending on the word.

Masculine
ὁ πόλεμος

the War

ὁ is the definite article for masculine nouns.

Feminine
ἡ ἡμέρα

the Day

ἡ is the definite article for feminine nouns.

Neuter
τό ἔργον

the Work

τό is the definite article for neuter nouns.
