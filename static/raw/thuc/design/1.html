Aspects of a Noun:
1. Gender
2. Number
3. Case

Every noun in Greek is considered to be of masculine, feminine, or neuter gender. The gender of a noun is an aspect of the word's grammar, and is not the same as biological sex.

You can identify the gender of a noun in two ways:

  1. The definite article ("the") which preceeds the word.
  2. The ending on the word.

Masculine
ὁ πόλεμος

the War

Feminine
ἡ ἡμέρα

the Day

Neuter
τό ἔργον

the Work
