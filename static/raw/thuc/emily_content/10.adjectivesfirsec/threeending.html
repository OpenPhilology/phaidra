Useful tip!
Unlike English verbs, the subject of the Greek verb is implied by the verb's ending. Therefore, it is not necessary for it to be accompanied by the personal pronouns I, You, He/She/It, We, You, They.

For example: The English I have can be translated either ἐγώ ἔχω or, more commonly, simply ἔχω.
