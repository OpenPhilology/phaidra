How First and Second Declension Adjectives work

Just like articles and nouns have gender, number and case, so do all the adjectives. Like the article, an adjective must agree in gender, number and case with the noun it describes.

However, this does not mean that the ending of the article and that of the noun are always identical; there are exceptions where, though the adjective and noun agree, the endings might look different (e.g. ἄδικος ψυχή, meaning an unjust soul).

Adjectives agreeing with first and second declension nouns fall into two groups:

  • Three-ending Adjectives
    There are separate sets of endings for each of the three genders.

  • Two-ending Adjectives
    Masculine and Feminine nouns share one set of endings, while neuter has a different set.
