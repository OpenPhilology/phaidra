Now in the Pentecontaetia...

Under the Athenian military leader, Cimon son of Miltiades, the Athenians and the allies together defeat the Persian once again on the river Eurymedon. The Athenians also manage to suppress the revolt of the Thracians by defeating them at sea and landing on their island. However, they are defeated in Amphipolis while they attempt to advance further into Thrace. The defeated Thracians appeal to the Spartans, who, in turn, promise to assist them by invading Attica, land of the Athenians. But their aid was prevented by a terrible earthquake and οἱ Ἀθηναῖοι, the Athenians, grow more and more unpopular as they demand monetary and military contributions from the allies. For this, οἱ ξύμμαχοι, the allies, have themselves to blame; wishing to get off service, they had decided to pay their share of the money in ships. Thus the Athenian navy was growing larger than ever before.

(Summary of Thuc. 1.97-98-99)
