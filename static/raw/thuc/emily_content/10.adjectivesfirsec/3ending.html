Three-ending Adjectives of the First and Second Declension

As you can see from the table, the adjective δίκαιος, δικαία, δίκαιον has the same endings as first declension nouns in the feminine, and as second declension nouns in the masculine and neuter.

Example: ὁ δίκαιος ἄνθρωπος, meaning the just man.

Word Order and Adjectives

The meaning given to the article, noun, and its agreeing adjective can vary slightly according to word order. So, you might not always find these three components (article, noun, adjective) in the same sequence.

For example: ὁ ἀγαθὸς ἄνθρωπος means the good man, but ἄνθρωπος ὁ ἀγαθος means a man, the good one
