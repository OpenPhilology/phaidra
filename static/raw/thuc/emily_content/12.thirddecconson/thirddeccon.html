Third declension: consonant stems

Third declension nouns can be masculine, feminine, or neuter. They are called "nouns with consonant stems" because their stems end with a consonant and to distinguish them from the third declension nouns with vowel stems.

Unlike nouns from the first and second declensions, the nominative singular forms of third declension nouns don't have a specific ending - rather, the ending of every noun is different. However, some features of the third declension remain stable for every noun. These are:
  • the endings of the genitive
  • the endings of the dative
  • the endings of the accusative plural
  • the endings of the vocative plural

Useful tip!

In the third declension, masculine and feminine nouns have the same endings.

Keep and eye out!

In masculine and feminine nouns, the vocative singular is identical in form to the nominative singular:

  • when the nominative singular ends in -ξ or -ψ
  • when the nominative singular ends in -ν or -ρ accents the ultima
Otherwise the nominative singular stays as the stem alone, with any final letter dropped (e.g.ἐλπι)
