The Dative Case

The word "dative" derives from the Latin word dare, meaning to "give". In most cases its meaning can be associated with the English words to and for. It goes naturally with verbs of giving, so if you take the sentence Thucydides gave a story to the audience, you will find "to the audience" in the dative.

These verbs are generally followed by a direct object in the accusative and an indirect object in the dative. Take a look at the example below!

ὁ ἀνήρ δίδωσι δῶρον τῇ κόρῃ.
The man gives a rose to the girl.
