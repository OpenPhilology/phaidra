Accusative

The accusative case is used to indicate the object of a sentence.

Remember! It helps to identify the main verb of a sentence first.

Examples:

Thucydides describes the war.

ὁ Θουκυδίδης ἐξηγέται τόν πόλεμον.

The city has an army.

ἡ πόλις ἔχει στρατίαν.

Useful tip! While there is a Greek transaltion for the definite article "the", there is no translation for the indefinite article "a" or "an". In this case, you can just skip the Greek article and translate the noun directly.
