The Nominative Case

Nominatives in the Pentecontaetia

Essentially every sentence in Greek contains a word in the nominative case. Take a look at the very beginning of Thucydides' Pentacontaetia!

οἱ γὰρ Ἀθηναῖοι τρόπῳ τοιῷδε ἦλθον...
So the Athenians, in this way, came...
(Subject)   (Verb)
— The Peloponnesian War, 1.89.1

Useful tip!
The Greek word order might surprise you. You will find that the word order doesn't follow particular conventions and the verb may often be at the end of a sentence!
Hunting for the verb first is a good way to figure out what kind of subject you're looking for — for instance, whether the subject must be singular or plural.
