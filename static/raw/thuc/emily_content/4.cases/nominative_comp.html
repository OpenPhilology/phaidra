The Nominative Case

Nominative as the Complement

The complement, which is also a noun or adjective in the nominative case, always follows the verb to be. Now you can see why spotting the verb first can help you understand the function of the other words in the sentence!

ἡ ἡμέρα ἐστὶ μακρά.
The day is long.
(Subject) (Linking Verb) (Complement)
ὁ Θουκυδίδης ἐστὶ συγγραφεύς
Thucydides is a historian.
(Subject) (Linking Verb) (Complement)
Σικελία ἐστὶ νῆσος
Sicily is an island.
(Subject) (Linking Verb) (Complement)
Useful tip!
The Greek word order might surprise you. You will find that the word order doesn't follow particular conventions and the verb may often be at the end of a sentence!
