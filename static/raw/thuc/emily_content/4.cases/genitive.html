The Genitive Case

In most cases the meaning of the genitive can be associated with the English word of, so if you take the sentence Thucydides wrote a narration of events, you will find "of events" in the genitive.

Useful tip!
The genitive is a very useful case. Very often the ending of the genitive will help you determine the gender and number of irregular nouns that in the nominative may confuse you. For example, we know the name "Thucydides" is masculine, though the ending Θουκυδίδης may look feminine. The genitive here, however, shows you that this noun is undoubtedly masculine: Θουκυδίδου.
