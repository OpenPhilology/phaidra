The Genitive Case

Genitive of Comparison

In these sentences, the genitive expresses comparison. When ἤ (than) is absent, the object of comparison (i.e. anything after 'than' in English) is in the genitive.

ὁ Θουκυδίδης ἐστὶ σώφρων τοῦ παιδός.
Thucydides is wiser than the boy.
(Nominative) (Verb) (Genitive)
Genitive following Prepositions

In these sentences, the genitive followsprepositions for a variety of purposes. Here is the example of the preposition ἐκ meaning out of/from.

ἡ κόρη ἐκβαίνει ἐκ τῆς οἰκίας.
The girl walks out of the house
