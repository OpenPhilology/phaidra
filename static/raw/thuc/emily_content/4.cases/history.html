Quick Recap!

So, you've learned that Thucydides is the author of the History of the Peloponnesian War, the story of the great ὁ πόλεμος, the war, fought between the Peloponnesian League, led by οἱ Λακεδαιμόνιοι, the Spartans, and the Delian League, led by οἱ ᾿Αθηναῖοι, the Athenians. As you go through the lessons you will become particularly familiar with one part of Thucydides' great historical account — a section of the first book called ἡ πεντηκονταετία, The Pentecontaetia.



Can you guess what Pentecontaetia means?

The word is a combination of πεντήκοντα meaning fifty and ετία meaning age. The fifty year age marks the period between the defeat of the second Persian invasion of Greece in 479 BC and the beginning of the Peloponnesian war in 433 BC, when Athens was gradually becoming the most powerful state in the Greek world. Athens was in fact building its empire and exercised control over the allies with its fierce ἡ στρατιά, its army, named the Delian League.
