Nominative

The word "nominative" originally comes from the Greek word ὄνομα, meaning "name". Thus, the nominative case is used to indicate the subject of a sentence or the complement of the verb.

Useful tip!
It helps to identify the main verb of a sentence first. Once you have found the verb, it becomes easier to understand the function of the other words in the sentence.
Nominative as the subject
Thucydides describes the war.
ἡ ἡμέρα εστίν μακρά.
The day is long.
Nominative as the complement
Thucydides is the narrator.
Σικελία εστίν νῆσος.
Sicily is an island.
The complement, which is also a noun in the nominative, always follows the verb to be. Now you can see why spotting the verb first can help you understand the function of the other words in the sentence!
