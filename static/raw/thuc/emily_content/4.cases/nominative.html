The Nominative Case

What's in a name?

The word "nominative" originally comes from the Greek word ὄνομα, meaning "name". Thus, the nominative case is used to indicate the subject of a sentence or the complement of the verb.

Keep an eye out!

Take a look at the table to your left. We'll be focusing mostly on words that fit into this pattern, or declension. Pay extra attention in this lesson to wherever you see words ending in -α or -αι, because you'll soon learn these words as part of your vocabulary!
