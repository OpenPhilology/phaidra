The Nominative Case

Nominative as the Subject

In these sentences, the subject of the sentence is marked by the nominative case.

ὁ Θουκυδίδης ἐξηγέται τόν πόλεμον.
Thucydides describes the war.
(Subject) (Verb) (Object)
ἡ ἡμέρα φέρει τήν ἡσυχίαν
The day brings rest.
(Subject) (Verb) (Object)
Useful tip!
You can always rely on the definite article, such as ὁ or ἡ in these examples, to indicate the nominative case.
