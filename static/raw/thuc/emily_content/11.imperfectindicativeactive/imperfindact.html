Imperfect Indicative Active

As you now know:

  • The imperfect tense describes an action in the past with progressive aspect (i.e. the action took place over a period of time).
  • The indicative mood is used for factual statements and questions.
  • The active voice means the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Examples of the Imperfect in English

I was grading exams, when suddenly I heard thunder.

Emily was drinking tea all morning.

The ships were sailing at top speed.

Notice how, in these examples, the verbs took place over a period of time — i.e. they express progressive aspect. If the sentences were simply "I graded exams", "Emily drank coffee", and "The ships sailed", their verbs would not belong in the Imperfect tense. This is because "graded", "drank", and "sailed" would indicate simple, one-time actions.

Tips

Keep an eye out!
Remember that unlike English verbs, the subject of the Greek verb is implied by the verb's ending. Therefore, it is not necessary for it to be accompanied by the personal pronouns such as I, You, He/She/It, We, You, They.
