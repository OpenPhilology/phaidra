Imperfect Indicative Active

As you now know:

  • Theimperfect tensedescribes an action in the past with progressive aspect (i.e. the action is still taking place).
  • Theindicative moodis used for factual statements and questions.
  • Theactive voicemeans the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Keep and eye out!
Remember that unlike English verbs, the subject of the Greek verb is implied by the verb's ending. Therefore, it is not necessary for it to be accompanied by the personal pronouns I, You, He/She/It, We, You, They.
