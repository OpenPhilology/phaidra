First declension

The first Greek declension consists of nearly exclusively feminine nouns. Only few masculine nouns belong to the first declension and no neuter nouns.

The first and most common group of nouns ends in -α in the whole singular and plural. Take a look at the declension of ἡ χώρα meaning the land.

Look out for Accents!

When a first declension noun has an acute accent on the last syllable in the nominative, the accent is changed to a circumflex in the genitive and dative, in both the singular and plural.

For example: ἡ στρατιά (meaning the army) will become τῆς στρατιᾶς in the genitive singular.
