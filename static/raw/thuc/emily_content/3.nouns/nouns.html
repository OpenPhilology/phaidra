How Greek Nouns Work

Just like many modern languages (for example, German and Russian) Greek is an inflected language, meaning that the endings of nouns, pronouns, adjectives and verbs change to reflect their relationship with other words.

Take, for example, the Greek word for "day":

ἡ ἡμέρα
The Day
By looking at ἡ ἡμέρα, you may already identify its 3 characteristics:

  1. Gender: Feminine
  2. Number: Singular
  3. Case: Nominative
