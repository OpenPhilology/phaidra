Case

The last thing to remember about Greek nouns is that their ending changes according to the case they are in. The case of a Greek noun indicates its grammatical relation to the rest of the sentence (i.e., subject, direct object, etc.)

Take the example: Thucydides describes the war.

Thucydides is the subject or nominative of the verb describes, while the war is the direct object or accusative of the sentence.

In Greek there are 5 main cases:
  • Nominative
  • Accusative
  • Genitive
  • Dative
  • Vocative

In Greek, the ending of a noun will always indicate which case it is in. Indeed, both the definite article of a noun and the noun's ending will vary according to case. The stem of the noun, however, always remains the same.
