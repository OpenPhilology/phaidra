Nominatives in Thucydides

The subject of a sentence belongs in the nominative case. Remember that the subject of the sentence is the noun which performs the action of the verb.

"[ἥδε] ἡ ἡμέρα1 τοῖς Ἕλλησι μεγάλων κακῶν ἄρξει."

Thucydides' History of the Peloponnesian War, 2.012.3
"[This is] the day1 [that] will be the beginning of great misfortunes to the Hellenes."

Important things to notice!
  1. The subject of the sentence, or the word in the nominative case, does not have to be the first word in the sentence.
  2. The verb, ἄρξει, means "it begins". Since this verb does not mean "to be" or "to become", there are no other nominatives in this sentence.
