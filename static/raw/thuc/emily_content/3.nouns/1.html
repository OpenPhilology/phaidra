Gender

Every noun in Greek is either masculine, feminine or neuter. The gender of a noun is an aspect of the word's grammar, so try not getting it confused with biological sex - often the two will not match!

As you learnt in the previous slide, you can identify the gender of a noun by its definite article preceeding the word.

Feminine
ἡ ἡμέρα

the Day

ἡ is the definite article for feminine nouns.

Masculine
ὁ πόλεμος

the War

ὁ is the definite article for masculine nouns.

Neuter
τό ἔργον

the Work

τό is the definite article for neuter nouns.
