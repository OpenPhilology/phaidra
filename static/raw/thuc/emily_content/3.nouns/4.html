What makes a Noun?

Nouns have three important characteristics:

  1. Gender: Masculine, Feminine, or Neuter
  2. Number: Singular or Plural
  3. Case: Nominative, Genitive, Dative, Accusative

Find these Characteristics in a Noun

When you see a noun, you can look at its parts to find out its gender, number, and case. For example:

ἡ (the) ἡμέρα (Day)

ἡμέρ

Definite Article
The definite article, ἡ ("the"), tells us ἡμέρα is a feminine noun. 		Stem
The stem, ἡμέρ-, stays constant as we change the noun's number and case. 		Ending
The ending, -α, changes to indicate the noun's number and case.
