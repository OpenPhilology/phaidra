Tense

The tense of a verb always conveys information about aspect and sometimes information about aspect and time.

What does tense tell us?

The time of a verb indicates whether the verb is present, past or future. For example I am, I was, I will be.

The aspect of a verb indicates how the situation occurs in time. For example, did the action occur once, over a period of time, or repeatedly? Aspect can often answer these kinds of questions.

Summary of the Tenses

In Greek there are 7 tenses. You'll learn about each one in detail later!

  1. Present
    Describes an action in the present time with progressive aspect (i.e. the action is still taking place).
    For example: I am fighting.

  2. Future
    Describes an action in the future time with either simple aspect (i.e. fixed moment in time), for example I will fight or progressive aspect.
    For example: I will be fighting.

  3. Perfect
    Describes an action in the present time with completed aspect (i.e. fixed moment in time that has come to an end).
    For example: I have fought.

  4. Pluperfect
    Describes an action in the past time with completed aspect.
    For example: I had fought.

  5. Future Perfect
    Describes an action in future time with completed aspect.
    For example: I shall have fought.

  6. Imperfect
    Describes an action in the past time with progressive aspect.
    For example: I was fighting.

  7. Aorist
    Describes an action in the past time with simple aspect.
    For example: I fell.
