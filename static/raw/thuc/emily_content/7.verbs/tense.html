Tense

The tense of a verb always conveys information about aspect and sometimes information about aspect and time.

The time of a verb indicates whether the verb is present, past or future. For example I am, I was, I will be.

The aspect of a verb indicates when the situation occurs in time.

In Greek there are 7 tenses:

  • Present tense: describes an action in the present time with progressive aspect (i.e. the action is still taking place). For example I am fighting
  • Future tense: describes an action in the future time with either simple aspect (i.e. fixed moment in time), for example I shall fight or progressive aspect, for example I shall be fighting
  • Perfect tense: describes an action in the present time with completed aspect (i.e. fixed moment in time that has come to an end). For example I have fought
  • Pluperfect tense: describes an action in the past time with completed aspect. For example I had fought
  • Future perfect tense: describes an action in future time with completed aspect. For example I shall have fought
  • Imperfect tense: describes an action in the past time with progressive aspect. For example I was fighting
  • Aorist tense: describes an action in the past time with simple aspect. For example I fell
