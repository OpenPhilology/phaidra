Voice

Voice defines how the subject of the verb is involved in the action of the verb. Greek has three voices:

  1. Active
    If the subject is performing the action, the voice is active.
    For example: I scare (someone).

  2. Passive
    If the subject is receiving the action from an outside agency, the voice is passive.
    For example: I am scared (by something).

  3. Middle
    If the subject is performing the action with personal involvement, the voice is middle.
    For example I am frightened.

You will learn each voice in depth later in the course.
