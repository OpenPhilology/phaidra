Mood

The mood of a verb indicates whether a sentence is a factual statement, hypothetical, wishful, a commanding statement and so on. In Greek there are four moods:

  1. Indicative
    Mood for factual statements and questions.
    For example: I am a soldier.

  2. Subjunctive
    Mood for hypothetical and other types of statements.
    For example: If I were a soldier....

  3. Optative
    Mood for conditional statements.
    For example: I may be a soldier.

  4. Imperative
    Mood for commanding statements.
    For example: Be a soldier!

You will learn each mood in depth later in the course.
