First Accent Rule: General

Accents can only appear over one of the last three syllables of a word, no matter how many syllables a word has. Each of the last three syllables has its own name, so that it is easier to define the position of an accent.

ἄν
θρω
πος
Third syllable from the end

ANTEPENULT


 Penultimate syllable

PENULT


 Last syllable

ULTIMA

This example shows how the acute accent of this word is on the only vowel in the antepenult syllable.

Keep and eye out!

Just like vowels, syllables can also be as defined long or short depending on which vowel/s form them. So, for example, a syllable containing a long α will be a long syllable.
