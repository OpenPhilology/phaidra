The Second Perfect and Pluperfect

Some verbs whose stem ends in a π-mute (π β φ) or a κ-mute (κ γ χ) add α and η instead of κα and κη, and aspirate the final letter of the stem, changing π and β to φ, and κ and γ to χ, as

πέμπω, πέπομφ-α (with change of ε of the stem to ο), ἐπεπόμφ-η. διώκω, δεδίωχ-α, ἐδεδιώχ-η. ἄγω, ἦχ-α, ἤχ-η.

The perfects and pluperfects just described (114) are called SECOND perfects and pluperfects.
